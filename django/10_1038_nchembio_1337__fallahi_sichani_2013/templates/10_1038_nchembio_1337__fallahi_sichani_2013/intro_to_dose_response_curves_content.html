Parametrizing perturbagen dose-response relationships

Dose-response curves are the basis for the most widespread measures of cellular response to perturbing agents and drugs ("perturbagens", for short). Such curves measure the magnitude of an effect (for example, cell viability) across a range of drug concentrations, and are typically represented graphically with log-linear plots. They are obtained by fitting experimentally measured values to a logistic sigmoidal function:

where D is drug concentration, E(D) is a measured response parameter (e.g. cell viability), E0 and E∞ are the top and bottom asymptotes of the response curve, EC50 is the concentration at half-maximal effect (hme), and HS (for "Hill Slope") is a slope parameter analogous to a Hill coefficient. In addition to the parameters that appear explicitly in the equation above, three measures of drug response derived from this equation are in common use: (i) IC50, or “potency”, the concentration of drug at which response is half its theoretical maximum (i.e. E(D)/E0 = 0.5); (ii) the value of the effect at the maximum concentration of drug tested (Emax = E(Dmax)); and (iii) the area under the dose-response curve (AUC), also known as the “activity area”.
